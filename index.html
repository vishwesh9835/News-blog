FIIs Set for Biggest Exit from Indian Equities: ₹1.58 Lakh Crore Withdrawn in 2025

Business • 5 min read • December 30, 2025

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) are set to mark their biggest-ever exit from Indian stocks in 2025.

They have pulled out a staggering ₹1,58,407 crore, making it the largest annual withdrawal since they started investing in the Indian market. This record outflow combines massive stock market sales of ₹2,31,990 crore, slightly offset by ₹73,583 crore in primary market investments up to December 27, as reported by Economic Times.

In 2024, while FIIs sold ₹1,21,210 crore through stock exchanges, they balanced this by investing ₹1,21,637 crore into the primary market, resulting in a slightly positive net flow.

Looking Ahead to 2026

Despite the record exit, analysts remain cautiously optimistic. A market "cleansing" could create attractive entry points, with hopes pinned on a 2026 rebound driven by earnings growth and currency stability.
